About Britain Airlines

Our Heritage

Founded in 1974 from the merger of British Overseas Airways Corporation and British European Airways, Britain Airlines carries the legacy of British aviation excellence. From the pioneering days of the Comet to the modern A350, we have consistently set the standard for air travel.

Our mission is simple: to connect the United Kingdom with the world while maintaining the highest standards of safety, service, and British hospitality. With our headquarters at London Heathrow, we operate from the heart of one of the world's most dynamic cities.

Today, Britain Airlines serves over 150 destinations across 80 countries, carrying more than 45 million passengers annually. Our diverse fleet of over 280 aircraft ranges from the versatile Twin Otter to the magnificent A380.

Our Journey Through Time

1974
The Merger
Britain Airlines formed from the merger of BOAC and BEA, creating the UK's first comprehensive international and domestic carrier.
1976
Concorde Era
Became one of only two airlines to operate the Concorde supersonic airliner, offering London-New York flights in under 3.5 hours.
1990s
Global Expansion
Expanded route network to Asia-Pacific and South America, establishing Heathrow as a true global hub.
2008
Terminal 5
Moved to the state-of-the-art Terminal 5 at Heathrow, designed exclusively for Britain Airlines operations.
2013
A380 Arrival
Introduced the Airbus A380, the world's largest passenger aircraft, on high-demand routes to Dubai and Singapore.
2019
A350 XWB
Welcomed the Airbus A350-1000, featuring the world's most advanced cabin design and fuel efficiency.
2024
50th Anniversary
Celebrated 50 years of connecting Britain to the world, with special retro liveries and commemorative flights.

Our Aircraft Fleet

Regional Fleet

DHC-6 Twin Otter

Turboprop
Capacity 19 Passengers
Range 1,427 km
Cruise Speed 267 km/h
Introduced 1965
The de Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter is a Canadian STOL (Short Takeoff and Landing) utility aircraft. Britain Airlines operates this legendary turboprop for remote island services and challenging airfields across the Scottish Highlands and Channel Islands. Its exceptional short-field performance and rugged reliability make it perfect for connecting smaller communities.

ATR 72

Regional
Capacity 72 Passengers
Range 1,528 km
Cruise Speed 511 km/h
Manufacturer ATR (France/Italy)
The ATR 72 is a twin-engine turboprop short-haul regional airliner developed in France and Italy. Britain Airlines utilizes the ATR 72-600 variant for domestic routes and short European hops. With its excellent fuel efficiency and low operating costs, it serves as the backbone of our regional network, connecting secondary cities with environmental responsibility.

CRJ-200

Regional Jet
Capacity 50 Passengers
Range 3,150 km
Cruise Speed 860 km/h
Manufacturer Bombardier
The Bombardier CRJ200 is a regional jet designed for short-haul flights. Britain Airlines deploys these efficient 50-seaters on thin routes where jet speed is required but demand doesn't justify larger aircraft. Its impressive range allows it to operate longer regional sectors while maintaining the economics that keep smaller routes viable.

Embraer E-175

Regional Jet
Capacity 76 Passengers
Range 4,074 km
Cruise Speed 890 km/h
Wingspan 26.0 m
The Embraer E-175 represents the perfect balance between regional efficiency and mainline comfort. Britain Airlines operates the E-175 on high-frequency business routes and slot-constrained airports like London City. With its 2-2 seating configuration, every passenger enjoys either a window or aisle seat, embodying our commitment to passenger comfort.
Narrowbody Fleet

Boeing 737

Short-Medium Haul
Capacity 162-210 Pax
Range 6,570 km
Variants 737-800/MAX 8
Engines CFM International
The Boeing 737 is the world's best-selling commercial jetliner. Britain Airlines operates the Next Generation and MAX variants, serving European holiday destinations and business routes. The 737's proven reliability and operating economics make it the workhorse of our short to medium-haul network, from Mediterranean resorts to Eastern European capitals.

Airbus A320

Short-Medium Haul
Capacity 150-180 Pax
Range 6,100 km
Variants A320ceo/neo
Features Fly-by-wire
The Airbus A320 family revolutionized single-aisle travel with its digital fly-by-wire controls and spacious cabin. Britain Airlines' A320neo aircraft feature fuel-efficient LEAP-1A engines and sharklet wingtips, reducing fuel consumption by 15%. These aircraft form the core of our European network, offering passengers wider seats and a quieter cabin experience.

Boeing 757

Medium Haul
Capacity 200-295 Pax
Range 7,250 km
Engines Rolls-Royce/P&W
Status Being Phased Out
The Boeing 757, often called the "flying pencil" due to its long fuselage, is a medium- to long-range narrowbody. Britain Airlines operates a reduced fleet of 757-200s primarily on high-density European routes and select transatlantic services. Known for its powerful engines and hot-and-high performance, this iconic aircraft is gradually being replaced by newer generation aircraft.
Widebody Fleet

Boeing 767

Medium Widebody
Capacity 181-375 Pax
Range 11,070 km
Variants 767-300ER
Configuration 2-3-2 Economy
The Boeing 767 was the first widebody twinjet to enter service. Britain Airlines utilizes the 767-300ER on medium-haul transatlantic routes and high-capacity Mediterranean services. Its unique 2-3-2 economy seating means only one middle seat per row, offering passengers greater comfort. The aircraft serves as a versatile bridge between our narrowbody and long-haul fleets.

Boeing 777

Long Haul
Capacity 301-396 Pax
Range 17,370 km
Variants 777-200ER/300ER
Engines GE90/Trent 800
The Boeing 777, or "Triple Seven," is the world's largest twinjet and a cornerstone of Britain Airlines' long-haul fleet. Our 777-300ER aircraft feature our flagship First Class suites and fully flat business class beds. Capable of flying London to Sydney non-stop, these workhorses serve our most prestigious routes to Asia, the Americas, and Australia with exceptional reliability.

Boeing 787 Dreamliner

Long Haul
Capacity 242-330 Pax
Range 14,010 km
Variants 787-9/10
Features Composite Materials
The Boeing 787 Dreamliner represents the future of sustainable long-haul travel. Constructed primarily of composite materials, it offers 20% better fuel efficiency than comparable aircraft. Britain Airlines' 787-9 features larger dimmable windows, lower cabin altitude, and higher humidity levels, reducing jet lag. These aircraft open new thin long-haul routes previously economically unviable.

Airbus A350 XWB

Ultra Long Haul
Capacity 300-410 Pax
Range 18,000 km
Variants A350-900/1000
Wingspan 64.8 m
The Airbus A350 XWB (Extra Wide Body) is the most modern widebody in Britain Airlines' fleet. Featuring the quietest cabin of any twin-aisle aircraft and revolutionary Airspace interior design, our A350-1000 operates our longest routes including London-Singapore and London-Santiago. The carbon fiber fuselage and advanced aerodynamics make it the most fuel-efficient widebody flying today.
Ultra Large Aircraft

Boeing 747

Jumbo Jet
Capacity 366-660 Pax
Range 14,320 km
Variants 747-400/8I
Nickname Queen of the Skies
The Boeing 747, the "Queen of the Skies," revolutionized air travel with its iconic hump. Britain Airlines operates the 747-400 and the advanced 747-8I on our highest-demand routes. The upper deck offers exclusive premium cabins, while the main deck provides spacious economy seating. Though being phased out in favor of twins, the 747 remains a symbol of aviation's golden age and Britain's aerospace heritage.

Airbus A380

Superjumbo
Capacity 469-615 Pax
Range 15,200 km
Decks Full Double Deck
Wingspan 79.8 m
The Airbus A380 is the world's largest passenger airliner and the flagship of Britain Airlines' fleet. Our A380s feature First Class suites, a business class club lounge, premium economy, and spacious economy across two full decks. Operating exclusively from London Heathrow to destinations like Dubai, Singapore, and Los Angeles, the A380 offers unparalleled space, comfort, and quietness in modern aviation.
